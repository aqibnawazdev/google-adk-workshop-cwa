# Rome Travel Guide

## Quick Facts

- **Country**: Italy
- **Language**: Italian (English spoken in tourist areas)
- **Currency**: Euro (EUR, €)
- **Time Zone**: Central European Time (CET, UTC+1; CEST UTC+2 in summer)
- **Emergency Number**: 112 (unified emergency number)
- **Dialing Code**: +39
- **Capital**: Rome is the capital of Italy
- **Population**: ~2.8 million (metropolitan area ~4.3 million)

## Visa Requirements

### Entry Rules by Nationality

Rome is part of Italy, which is in the **Schengen Area**. Visa requirements depend on your nationality:

- **US Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism/business up to **90 days within any 180-day period**
- **UK Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism/business up to **90 days within any 180-day period** (post-Brexit)
- **Canadian Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism/business up to **90 days within any 180-day period**
- **Australian Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism/business up to **90 days within any 180-day period**
- **EU/EEA Citizens**: No visa required, freedom of movement
- **Other Nationals**: Check [Schengen visa requirements](https://ec.europa.eu/home-affairs/policies/schengen-borders-and-visa_en)

### Important Requirements

- **Passport Validity**: Must be valid for at least **3 months beyond your planned departure** from the Schengen area
- **Proof of Accommodation**: May be requested at immigration
- **Return Ticket**: May be requested to demonstrate intention to leave
- **Travel Insurance**: Recommended (required for some visa categories)
- **Sufficient Funds**: May need to demonstrate financial means (~€50-70 per day)

### ETIAS (Starting 2025)

Even visa-exempt travelers will need **ETIAS authorization** before travel to Schengen countries. Apply online before departure.

## Best Time to Visit

### Spring (April - May)

- **Weather**: Mild and pleasant (15-22°C / 59-72°F)
- **Pros**: Perfect weather, flowers blooming, Easter celebrations, fewer crowds than summer
- **Cons**: Easter week can be very crowded, occasional rain
- **Best for**: Sightseeing, walking tours, outdoor dining

### Summer (June - August)

- **Weather**: Hot and sunny (25-35°C / 77-95°F)
- **Pros**: Long days, lively atmosphere, outdoor events and concerts
- **Cons**: Very hot (especially July-August), extremely crowded, higher prices, many locals on vacation in August
- **Best for**: Those who love heat, evening activities, rooftop bars

### Fall (September - October)

- **Weather**: Excellent (18-26°C / 64-79°F in September, cooling to 13-20°C in October)
- **Pros**: Great weather, wine harvest season, fewer tourists than summer, cultural events
- **Cons**: Can be rainy in late October, some restaurants close for holidays
- **Best for**: Wine lovers, food tours, comfortable sightseeing

### Winter (November - February)

- **Weather**: Mild but can be rainy (8-15°C / 46-59°F)
- **Pros**: Very few tourists, lower prices, authentic local experience, Christmas decorations
- **Cons**: Shorter days, some rain, cooler temperatures, some sites have reduced hours
- **Best for**: Budget travelers, museum lovers, avoiding crowds

**Peak Season**: April-June, September-October
**Shoulder Season**: March, November
**Off Season**: December-February (except Christmas/New Year)

## Neighborhoods & Districts

### Centro Storico (Historic Center)

- **Character**: Heart of ancient and Renaissance Rome, pedestrian-friendly maze of piazzas
- **Must-See**: Pantheon, Piazza Navona, Campo de' Fiori, Trevi Fountain
- **Food**: Traditional Roman trattorias, gelaterias, cafes
- **Transport**: Mostly walkable, nearest metro: Spagna, Barberini
- **Stay Here If**: You want to be in the middle of everything

### Trastevere

- **Character**: Bohemian neighborhood with cobblestone streets, artisan workshops, vibrant nightlife
- **Must-See**: Santa Maria in Trastevere church, local trattorias, street artists
- **Food**: Authentic Roman cuisine, aperitivo bars, artisan pizza
- **Transport**: Tram 8, bus H from Termini
- **Stay Here If**: You want authentic local atmosphere and great dining

### Vatican & Prati

- **Character**: Upscale residential area near Vatican City, elegant streets
- **Must-See**: Vatican Museums, St. Peter's Basilica, Castel Sant'Angelo
- **Food**: High-end restaurants, local markets, quality trattorias
- **Transport**: Metro A (Ottaviano, Cipro)
- **Stay Here If**: You want quieter area near Vatican, good value hotels

### Testaccio

- **Character**: Working-class neighborhood turned foodie haven, authentic and unpretentious
- **Must-See**: Testaccio Market, Protestant Cemetery, Monte Testaccio nightlife
- **Food**: Traditional Roman offal dishes, food market, aperitivo culture
- **Transport**: Metro B (Piramide), tram 3
- **Stay Here If**: You're a food lover seeking authentic Rome

### Monti

- **Character**: Hipster neighborhood with vintage shops, craft beer bars, creative vibe
- **Must-See**: Monti district streets, boutique shopping, Colosseum nearby
- **Food**: Trendy restaurants, wine bars, international cuisine
- **Transport**: Metro B (Cavour, Colosseo)
- **Stay Here If**: You want trendy atmosphere near ancient sites

### Aventine Hill

- **Character**: Quiet, upscale residential area with stunning views and gardens
- **Must-See**: Keyhole view of St. Peter's (Knights of Malta), Orange Garden (Giardino degli Aranci)
- **Food**: Limited restaurants, more residential
- **Transport**: Metro B (Circo Massimo)
- **Visit For**: Peaceful escape, romantic sunset views

## Food & Dining

### Signature Roman Dishes

- **Cacio e Pepe**: Pasta with pecorino cheese and black pepper (deceptively simple, hard to perfect)
- **Carbonara**: Pasta with guanciale (cured pork jowl), egg, pecorino, black pepper (NO CREAM!)
- **Amatriciana**: Pasta with guanciale, tomato, pecorino
- **Gricia**: Pasta with guanciale and pecorino (like carbonara without egg)
- **Saltimbocca alla Romana**: Veal with prosciutto and sage
- **Carciofi alla Romana**: Roman-style artichokes
- **Supplì**: Fried rice balls with mozzarella (Roman version of arancini)
- **Pizza al Taglio**: Pizza by the slice, sold by weight
- **Porchetta**: Roasted pork with herbs

### Dining Customs

- **Coffee Culture**:
  - Cappuccino ONLY for breakfast (never after 11am!)
  - Espresso after meals, standing at the bar
  - Sitting at table costs more than standing at bar
- **Meal Times**:
  - Lunch: 12:30-15:00 (many places close after)
  - Dinner: 19:30-23:00 (restaurants open around 19:00)
  - Avoid tourist trap restaurants serving "all day"
- **Coperto**: Cover charge (€1-3 per person) for bread and table service - normal practice
- **Tipping**: Service often included; round up or leave 5-10% for exceptional service
- **Water**: Ask for "acqua del rubinetto" (tap water, free) or bottled water (€2-4)

### Where to Eat

- **Avoid**: Restaurants with picture menus, aggressive touts, those right on major tourist squares
- **Seek**: Trattorias where locals eat, no English menu (good sign!), daily specials on chalkboard
- **Price Guide**:
  - Budget meal: €10-15 (pizza al taglio, trattoria)
  - Mid-range: €25-40 per person
  - Fine dining: €60+ per person

### Aperitivo Culture

- **What**: Pre-dinner drinks (18:00-21:00) with complimentary snacks/buffet
- **Price**: €8-12 for drink + food
- **Where**: Bars in Trastevere, Monti, Testaccio
- **Tip**: Can substitute for dinner on a budget

## Transportation

### From the Airports

#### Fiumicino (FCO) - Leonardo da Vinci Airport

- **Leonardo Express Train**: Direct to Termini station (32 min, €14, every 15-30 min, 6:23-23:23)
- **FL1 Regional Train**: To Trastevere, Ostiense, Tiburtina (slower, stops at multiple stations, €8)
- **Bus**: Terravision/SIT to Termini (55 min, €6-7)
- **Taxi**: Fixed rate €48 to within Aurelian Walls, €55-60 outside, 45-60 min
- **Uber/Private Transfer**: Similar to taxi prices

#### Ciampino (CIA) Airport

- **Bus**: Terravision/SIT to Termini (40 min, €6)
- **Bus + Metro**: Local bus to Ciampino station, then train to Termini (cheaper but slower)
- **Taxi**: Fixed rate €30 to within Aurelian Walls, 30-40 min

### Public Transportation

#### Metro (Subway)

- **Lines**: Line A (orange), Line B (blue), Line C (green, limited)
- **Coverage**: Limited compared to other capitals, doesn't reach many tourist sites
- **Hours**: 5:30-23:30 (Fri-Sat until 1:30)
- **Tickets**: Single ride €1.50 (100 min), day pass €7, 3-day €18
- **Key Stops**: Termini (hub), Spagna (Spanish Steps), Colosseo, Ottaviano (Vatican)

#### Buses & Trams

- **Coverage**: Extensive network, reaches areas metro doesn't
- **Frequency**: Can be unreliable, check Google Maps for real-time
- **Night Buses**: After metro closes
- **Same Tickets**: Use same tickets as metro
- **Tip**: Confusing for tourists, but necessary for some destinations

#### Walking

- **Best Option**: Rome's historic center is compact and walkable
- **Challenges**: Cobblestones (wear comfortable shoes), traffic doesn't always stop for pedestrians
- **Recommended**: Walk as much as possible, use public transport for longer distances

#### Taxis & Ride-Sharing

- **Official Taxis**: White with taxi sign, metered (starting fare €3 weekday day, €4.50 night/Sunday)
- **Where to Find**: Taxi stands (not hailed on street), call radio taxi
- **Apps**: FreeNow (mytaxi), iTaxi
- **Uber**: Available but limited (UberBlack only, similar price to taxi)
- **Warning**: Avoid unlicensed taxis at airports/stations

### Roma Pass

- **72 Hours (€52)**: First 2 attractions free, then discounts + unlimited public transport
- **48 Hours (€32)**: First attraction free, then discounts + unlimited public transport
- **Worth It If**: Visiting 2+ major paid attractions + using public transport extensively

## Cultural Tips & Customs

### Dress & Appearance

- **Religious Sites**: Covered shoulders and knees required (Vatican, churches)
- **Bring a Scarf**: Women can cover shoulders quickly when entering churches
- **Romans Dress Well**: Smart casual is the norm, avoid beach/gym wear in city
- **Fare Bella Figura**: "Making a good impression" is important - dress presentably

### Social Customs

- **Greetings**: Handshake or kiss on both cheeks (friends/family)
- **Afternoon Riposo**: Many shops close 13:00-16:00 for siesta (less common in tourist areas)
- **Evening Passeggiata**: Evening stroll before dinner, social ritual
- **Slower Pace**: Don't rush meals or conversations, Romans take their time
- **Speaking Italian**: Learn basic phrases - locals appreciate the effort

### Dining Etiquette

- **No Cappuccino After Meals**: Considered a breakfast drink only
- **Break Bread, Don't Cut**: Tear bread with hands, don't use knife
- **Pasta Course First**: Traditional meal: antipasto, primo (pasta), secondo (meat/fish), dolce (dessert)
- **Don't Ask for Changes**: Chefs prepare dishes traditionally, customization frowned upon
- **When in Rome**: Follow local customs - if locals are eating pizza with hands, do the same

### Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Validate your public transport ticket (or face €100 fine)
- Keep right on escalators
- Dress modestly for churches
- Learn basic Italian phrases
- Take your time and enjoy the pace

**DON'T:**
- Sit on monument steps (illegal, can be fined)
- Eat/drink near fountains (Trevi Fountain especially - €500 fine)
- Touch artifacts or artwork
- Feed pigeons
- Assume everyone speaks English (try Italian first)

### Language Tips

- **Buongiorno**: Good morning/day (formal greeting)
- **Buonasera**: Good evening (after ~2pm)
- **Per favore**: Please
- **Grazie**: Thank you
- **Scusi**: Excuse me
- **Parla inglese?**: Do you speak English?
- **Il conto, per favore**: The bill, please

## Safety & Health

### Safety Overview

Rome is generally safe for tourists, but pickpocketing and petty theft are common in tourist areas.

### Areas to Watch

- **Termini Station**: Hub for pickpockets and scammers
- **Metro Lines A & B**: Crowded trains attract pickpockets
- **Colosseum/Forum Area**: Watch bags and pockets
- **Trevi Fountain**: Very crowded, prime pickpocket location
- **Buses**: Especially crowded buses (40, 64 to Vatican)

### Common Scams

- **Fake Petition Scam**: Women ask you to sign petition, then demand "donation" while accomplice steals from bag
- **Bracelet Scam**: Someone ties bracelet on wrist, then demands payment
- **Gladiator Photos**: Costumed men at Colosseum demand €20+ for photos
- **Restaurant Menus**: Some switch menus after you order (bring different bill)
- **Taxi Scams**: Unlicensed taxis at airports, "broken" meters
- **ATM Helpers**: Strangers offering to help at ATMs (decline)

### Safety Tips

- **Bags**: Crossbody bags in front, not backpacks in crowds
- **Valuables**: Don't carry more than needed, use hotel safe
- **Phone**: Be alert when using phone on street (snatch theft on scooters)
- **Stay Aware**: Especially in crowded areas and on public transport
- **Cobblestones**: Wear sturdy shoes with good ankle support

### Health & Medical

- **Emergency**: 112 (all emergencies)
- **Pharmacies**: Green cross sign, can advise on minor ailments
- **Prescription Drugs**: Bring sufficient supply + prescription
- **EU Health Card**: EU citizens should bring European Health Insurance Card
- **Travel Insurance**: Non-EU visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance
- **Tap Water**: Safe to drink from taps and public fountains (nasoni)
- **Drinking Fountains**: Nasoni fountains throughout city have drinkable water (free!)
- **Heat**: Stay hydrated in summer, use sunscreen, take breaks in heat

### Emergency Numbers

- **General Emergency**: 112
- **Police**: 113
- **Medical Emergency**: 118
- **Fire**: 115
- **Embassy/Consulate**: Keep your country's contact info

## Practical Information

### Electricity

- **Voltage**: 230V, 50Hz
- **Plug Types**: Type C (2-pin), Type F (2-pin), Type L (3-pin Italian)
- **Adapter Needed**: Most countries need adapter
- **Where to Buy**: Airports, electronics shops, some hotels provide

### Money & Banking

- **Currency**: Euro (EUR, €)
- **ATMs**: Widely available, best exchange rates
- **Credit Cards**: Visa/Mastercard widely accepted, American Express less so
- **Cash**: Still preferred at small shops, markets, cafes
- **Daily Budget**: Budget €60-80, mid-range €120-180, luxury €300+
- **Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated - round up or 5-10%

### Pharmacies (Farmacia)

- **Marked By**: Green cross sign
- **Services**: Over-the-counter medication, basic health advice
- **After Hours**: Rotating 24-hour pharmacies (list posted on each pharmacy)
- **Prescription**: Some medications available without prescription that require one elsewhere

### August Closures (Ferragosto)

- **When**: Mid-August (especially around August 15)
- **What Closes**: Many family-owned restaurants, some shops
- **What Stays Open**: Major tourist attractions, hotels, restaurants in tourist areas
- **Plan Ahead**: Check opening hours if visiting mid-August

### Business Hours

- **Shops**: Mon-Sat 9:00/10:00 - 13:00, 15:30/16:00 - 19:30/20:00
- **Sunday**: Many shops closed (tourist areas often open)
- **Restaurants**: Lunch 12:30-15:00, dinner 19:30-23:00
- **Museums**: Vary, often closed Mondays
- **Churches**: Often close 12:00-15:00 for siesta

### Internet & Communication

- **SIM Cards**: Available at airports, tobacco shops (tabacchi), phone stores
- **Providers**: TIM, Vodafone, Wind Tre
- **Tourist SIMs**: €15-25 for 2-4 weeks with data
- **Free WiFi**: Common in hotels, cafes, some piazzas (Roma WiFi network)

### Useful Apps

- **Transport**: Google Maps, Moovit, Roma Mobilità
- **Food**: TheFork (restaurant reservations/discounts), Tripadvisor
- **Translation**: Google Translate (download Italian offline)
- **Taxis**: FreeNow (mytaxi), iTaxi

### Public Toilets

- **Limited**: Not many free public toilets
- **Options**: Cafes/bars (order espresso €1), department stores, museums
- **McDonald's**: Reliable option with bathrooms
- **Tip**: Use bathroom at attractions before leaving

### Shopping

- **Markets**: Campo de' Fiori (morning, daily except Sunday), Porta Portese (Sunday flea market)
- **Shopping Streets**: Via del Corso, Via Condotti (luxury)
- **Department Stores**: Rinascente, Coin
- **VAT Refund**: Non-EU residents can claim VAT back (minimum €154.94 purchase)

---

**Travel Responsibly**: Rome is a living city and UNESCO World Heritage site. Respect monuments, follow rules, support local businesses, and help preserve this incredible city for future generations.

**Pro Tip**: The best way to experience Rome is to wander. Get lost in the narrow streets, stumble upon hidden piazzas, and let the city reveal itself to you. Some of the most magical moments happen when you're not following a guidebook.
