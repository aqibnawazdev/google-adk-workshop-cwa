# Paris Travel Guide

## Quick Facts

- **Country:** France
- **Language:** French (English spoken in tourist areas, learning basic French appreciated)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €)
- **Time Zone:** Central European Time (CET, UTC+1), CEST in summer (UTC+2)
- **Emergency Numbers:** Universal emergency 112, Police 17, Fire 18, Ambulance 15
- **Dialing Code:** +33
- **Capital:** Paris (City of Light, La Ville Lumière)

- Valid passport (must be valid for 3 months beyond stay)

## Best Time to Visit

### Spring (April - June)
- **Weather:** 12-20°C (54-68°F), mild and pleasant
- **Highlights:** Gardens in bloom, terraces reopen, fewer crowds than summer
- **Events:** French Open tennis (May-June), Nuit des Musées (Museum Night, May)
- **Pros:** Beautiful weather, manageable crowds, outdoor cafe culture thrives
- **Cons:** Some rain showers, prices rising in June
- **Best Months:** April-May (ideal weather before summer rush)

### Summer (July - August)
- **Weather:** 20-25°C (68-77°F), warm and sunny
- **Highlights:** Bastille Day fireworks (July 14), Paris Plages (beach on Seine)
- **Crowds:** Very high, peak tourist season
- **Events:** Bastille Day, Paris Plages, summer music festivals
- **Pros:** Long daylight hours (sunset ~10pm), vibrant atmosphere, festivals
- **Cons:** Very crowded, many Parisians on holiday (some shops closed August), higher prices

### Fall (September - November)
- **Weather:** 10-20°C (50-68°F), crisp and cool
- **Highlights:** Autumn foliage, harvest season, cultural events resume
- **Events:** Paris Fashion Week (September), Nuit Blanche (all-night art event, October)
- **Pros:** Comfortable weather, fewer tourists than summer, cultural calendar full
- **Cons:** Rain increases, days getting shorter
- **Best Months:** September-October (warm enough, less crowded)

### Winter (December - February)
- **Weather:** 3-8°C (37-46°F), cold and gray
- **Highlights:** Christmas markets, New Year on Champs-Élysées, winter sales (January)
- **Events:** Christmas markets, New Year's Eve, Fashion Week (January)
- **Pros:** Lowest prices, festive atmosphere, no crowds at museums, cozy cafes
- **Cons:** Cold and rainy, short days, some attractions reduced hours

**Recommended Visit:** April-May or September-October for best balance of weather, crowds, and prices.

**Avoid:** August (many businesses closed, extremely crowded at major sites).

## Arrondissements (Districts)

Paris is divided into 20 arrondissements (districts) spiraling clockwise from the center.

### 1st Arrondissement (Louvre)
- **Character:** Historic heart, royal palace area, upscale shopping
- **Must-See:** Louvre Museum, Tuileries Garden, Palais Royal
- **Food:** High-end restaurants, historic cafes, gourmet shops
- **Transport:** Palais-Royal, Louvre-Rivoli (Métro 1)
- **Best For:** Art museums, luxury shopping, historic Paris

### 4th Arrondissement (Marais)
- **Character:** Trendy neighborhood, historic Jewish quarter, LGBTQ+ friendly
- **Must-See:** Notre-Dame, Île de la Cité, Place des Vosges, Centre Pompidou
- **Food:** Falafel (Rue des Rosiers), trendy bistros, wine bars
- **Transport:** Saint-Paul, Hôtel de Ville (Métro 1)
- **Best For:** Boutiques, nightlife, historic architecture, falafel

### 5th Arrondissement (Latin Quarter)
- **Character:** Student quarter, intellectual hub, bookshops and cafes
- **Must-See:** Panthéon, Luxembourg Gardens, Shakespeare and Company bookstore
- **Food:** Traditional bistros, international cuisine, student-friendly prices
- **Transport:** Saint-Michel, Cluny-La Sorbonne (RER B, Métro 10)
- **Best For:** Bookstores, student atmosphere, affordable dining

### 6th Arrondissement (Saint-Germain-des-Prés)
- **Character:** Literary cafes, art galleries, upscale bohemian
- **Must-See:** Luxembourg Gardens, Café de Flore, Les Deux Magots
- **Food:** Classic Parisian cafes, patisseries, fine dining
- **Transport:** Saint-Germain-des-Prés, Odéon (Métro 4, 10)
- **Best For:** Cafe culture, shopping, literary history

### 7th Arrondissement (Eiffel Tower)
- **Character:** Residential elegance, government buildings, iconic landmarks
- **Must-See:** Eiffel Tower, Musée d'Orsay, Invalides, Rodin Museum
- **Food:** Traditional French restaurants, boulangeries, market streets
- **Transport:** Trocadéro, Bir-Hakeim (Métro 6)
- **Best For:** Classic Paris landmarks, museums, elegant neighborhoods

### 18th Arrondissement (Montmartre)
- **Character:** Artistic heritage, hilltop village feel, nightlife (Pigalle)
- **Must-See:** Sacré-Cœur, Place du Tertre (artists square), Moulin Rouge
- **Food:** Traditional bistros, crepe stands, ethnic restaurants
- **Transport:** Abbesses, Anvers (Métro 2, 12)
- **Best For:** Bohemian atmosphere, views, street art, cabaret

## Food & Dining

### Signature French Foods to Try

#### Bread and Pastries
- **Baguette:** Fresh daily (€1-2), crispy crust essential
- **Croissant:** Buttery, flaky breakfast staple (€1.50-3)
- **Pain au Chocolat:** Chocolate-filled pastry (€1.50-3)
- **Macarons:** Delicate almond cookies, Ladurée famous (€2-3 each)
- **Éclair:** Cream-filled pastry with chocolate/coffee glaze

#### Classic Dishes
- **Steak Frites:** Steak with french fries (€18-30)
- **Coq au Vin:** Chicken braised in red wine (€16-25)
- **Boeuf Bourguignon:** Beef stew in Burgundy wine (€18-28)
- **Escargots:** Snails in garlic butter (€12-18 for 6-12)
- **French Onion Soup:** Caramelized onions, beef broth, melted cheese (€8-12)
- **Duck Confit:** Slow-cooked duck leg (€20-32)
- **Crêpes:** Sweet or savory thin pancakes (€4-10)

#### Cheese and Charcuterie
- **Cheese Plate:** Selection of French cheeses (€12-18)
- **Charcuterie Board:** Cured meats, pâté, terrines (€14-20)
- **Raclette/Fondue:** Melted cheese dishes (winter specialty, €18-25)

### Dining Culture

#### Boulangeries (Bakeries)
- Fresh bread and pastries daily
- Buy baguette in morning or before 6pm (freshest)
- Eat within 24 hours (no preservatives)

#### Cafés
- **Café Culture:** Sit and people-watch for hours (no rush)
- **Pricing:** Cheaper at counter (comptoir) vs table (terrasse)
- **Coffee:** "Café" = espresso, "Café crème" = coffee with milk, "Noisette" = espresso with milk
- **Breakfast:** Continental (coffee, croissant, juice) €8-15

#### Bistros and Brasseries
- **Bistro:** Small, casual restaurant with traditional French food
- **Brasserie:** Larger, serves food all day, more extensive menu
- **Prix Fixe Menu:** Set menu (entrée, plat, dessert) better value (€25-45)
- **À la Carte:** Individual dishes (more expensive)

#### Markets
- **Food Markets:** Fresh produce, cheese, meats, bread (morning hours)
- **Best Markets:** Marché Bastille (Thu/Sun), Marché d'Aligre (daily except Mon)
- **Picnic:** Buy at market, enjoy in park (quintessential Paris experience)

### Tipping and Service
- **Service Compris:** Service charge included in bill (15%)
- **Extra Tip:** Small coins or round up (€1-5) for good service (optional)
- **No Obligation:** Not required like in US, but appreciated

### Price Ranges
- **Budget (€10-20/meal):** Boulangeries, crêperies, ethnic food, market picnics
- **Mid-Range (€25-45/meal):** Bistros, brasseries, prix fixe menus
- **High-End (€60-150+/meal):** Fine dining, Michelin-starred restaurants, tasting menus

### Dining Etiquette
- **Greeting:** Say "Bonjour" when entering, "Au revoir" when leaving
- **Water:** Ask for "carafe d'eau" (free tap water) vs expensive bottled
- **Bread:** Free at dinner, place on table not plate
- **Hands:** Keep both hands visible on table (not in lap)
- **Pace:** Meals are leisurely (2-3 hours normal), don't rush
- **Bill:** Ask for "l'addition s'il vous plaît" (won't bring until requested)

## Transportation

### From Airports

#### Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG)
- **Location:** 25 km northeast of Paris (45-60 minutes to central Paris)
- **RER B Train:** Direct to central Paris (€11.45, 30-45 min to Gare du Nord/Châtelet)
- **RoissyBus:** Direct to Opéra (€16.60, 60-75 min)
- **Le Bus Direct:** Luxury coach to Eiffel Tower/Gare de Lyon (€18, 60-90 min)
- **Taxi:** Fixed rates to central Paris (€50-55 right bank, €58-65 left bank)
- **Uber:** €45-70 depending on time of day and traffic

#### Orly Airport (ORY)
- **Location:** 13 km south of Paris (30-40 minutes to central Paris)
- **OrlyBus:** To Denfert-Rochereau (€11.50, 30 min), connect to Métro
- **RER B+Orlyval:** Automated train to RER B (€14.50, 35 min total)
- **Tram 7:** To Villejuif station (€2.15, connect to Métro, slower but cheap)
- **Taxi:** Fixed rates (€35-41 right bank, €30-38 left bank)

### Métro and Public Transport

#### System Overview
- **Métro:** 16 lines (numbered), 300+ stations, extensive coverage
- **RER:** 5 suburban lines (A-E), connects city center to suburbs
- **Bus:** 60+ routes, scenic but slower than Métro
- **Tram:** 11 tram lines, mostly outer areas
- **Hours:** ~5:30am-12:40am (2am on Fridays/Saturdays), Night buses after

#### Tickets and Passes
- **Single Ticket (t+):** €2.15 (valid 90 min, transfers allowed within Métro/bus)
- **Carnet:** 10 tickets for €17.35 (save €4)
- **Navigo Easy:** Reloadable card (€2 one-time, load t+ tickets or day passes)
- **Paris Visite:** Unlimited travel 1-5 days (€13.95-€74.50, includes airport)
- **Navigo Week Pass:** Unlimited Mon-Sun (€30, photo required, locals use)
- **Phone Apps:** Bonjour RATP app for routes, RATP ticket app for mobile tickets

#### Navigation Tips
- **Direction:** Follow line number and end station direction (e.g., "Métro 1 direction La Défense")
- **Transfers:** Follow "Correspondance" signs for line changes
- **Exits:** "Sortie" = exit, check posted neighborhood map for correct exit
- **Validate:** Insert ticket in yellow machines (keep until exiting station)
- **Peak Hours:** Avoid 8-9:30am and 5:30-7pm (very crowded)

### Walking
- **Walkability:** Paris is compact and walkable (1st-20th arrondissements)
- **Seine Walks:** Beautiful riverside strolls, bridges connect islands
- **Safety:** Watch for pickpockets in tourist areas, aggressive traffic
- **Distances:** Most arrondissements 20-40 min walk between them

### Biking
- **Vélib':** Public bike share (€5/day pass, €5/month subscription)
- **Stations:** 1,400+ stations throughout Paris
- **Bike Lanes:** Improving, but share road with cars often
- **Seine Banks:** Car-free riverside paths perfect for cycling

### Taxis and Rideshare
- **Taxis:** Metered, expensive (€7 base + €1.15/km), hard to hail (use taxi stands)
- **Uber:** Available, comparable pricing to taxis
- **G7 Taxi App:** Official taxi app (English available)
- **Night Surcharge:** 17:00-10:00 and Sundays/holidays (higher rates)

## Cultural Tips & Customs

### Language and Greetings
- **Basic French Essential:** Always start with "Bonjour" (hello) or "Bonsoir" (evening)
- **Effort Appreciated:** Attempting French is valued, even if you switch to English
- **Essential Phrases:**
  - "Bonjour" (hello - before 6pm)
  - "Bonsoir" (hello - after 6pm)
  - "S'il vous plaît" (please)
  - "Merci" (thank you)
  - "Parlez-vous anglais?" (Do you speak English?)
  - "Excusez-moi" (excuse me)
  - "Je ne comprends pas" (I don't understand)
- **Formality:** Use "vous" (formal you) with strangers, not "tu" (informal)

### Social Etiquette
- **Greetings:** Light kiss on each cheek (la bise) among friends, handshake for business
- **Eye Contact:** Direct eye contact expected in conversations
- **Loud Behavior:** Parisians speak quietly in public, avoid loud American conversations
- **Punctuality:** Arrive on time for reservations (10-15 min late socially acceptable)
- **Dress Code:** Parisians dress elegantly, avoid athletic wear in restaurants
- **No Eating on the Go:** Sit down to eat, not while walking (exception: gelato)

### Shopping and Markets
- **Boutique Hours:** Often 10:00-19:00, closed Sundays and sometimes Mondays
- **Sales Periods:** January and July (les soldes - 30-70% off)
- **Greeting:** Say "Bonjour" when entering shop, "Au revoir" when leaving
- **Touching Products:** Ask before touching in small shops ("Je peux?")
- **Returns:** Strict policies, inspect before buying

### Tipping
- **Restaurants:** Service included (service compris), round up or €1-5 optional
- **Cafés:** Round up at counter, €1-2 if table service
- **Taxis:** Round up to nearest euro or add €1-2
- **Hotels:** €1-2 per bag for porter, €5-10/day for housekeeping
- **Tour Guides:** €5-10 for half day, €10-20 for full day

### Public Behavior
- **Dog-Friendly:** Dogs allowed in many restaurants and shops (watch sidewalks!)
- **Public Restrooms:** Limited and often cost €0.50-2, use cafes/museums
- **Smoking:** Common at outdoor terraces, banned indoors
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people, Eiffel at night protected

## Safety & Health

### General Safety
- **Crime Level:** Moderate, mostly petty theft and pickpocketing
- **Tourist Areas:** High pickpocket risk at Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Sacré-Cœur, Métro lines 1 & 4
- **Scams:** Petition scams, ring scams, friendship bracelet scams (be firm, walk away)
- **Night Safety:** Generally safe in central areas, avoid isolated parks and some outer arrondissements
- **Strikes:** Common (transport, museums), check news before visiting

### Pickpocket Prevention
- **Target Areas:** Crowded Métro (especially line 1), tourist attractions, markets
- **Tactics:** Distraction (spilled drink, signatures), bag slashing, phone snatching
- **Prevention:** Front pockets, cross-body bags, zipped closed, don't use phone on Métro platform
- **Valuables:** Leave in hotel safe, carry minimal cash

### Emergency Services
- **Universal Emergency:** 112 (English available)
- **Police:** 17
- **Ambulance:** 15 (SAMU)
- **Fire:** 18
- **Tourist Police:** 3 Rue de la Cité (near Notre-Dame), English spoken

### Medical Care
- **Healthcare Quality:** Excellent, but not free for tourists
- **Travel Insurance:** Essential (can be expensive)
- **Pharmacies:** Green cross sign, can provide basic medical advice
- **24-Hour Pharmacy:** Pharmacie des Champs-Élysées, 84 Avenue des Champs-Élysées
- **English-Speaking Hospitals:** American Hospital of Paris (Neuilly), Hertford British Hospital

### Water and Food Safety
- **Tap Water:** Safe to drink (fountains marked "eau potable")
- **Food Safety:** High standards, safe to eat anywhere
- **Allergies:** Inform restaurants ("Je suis allergique à...")

## Practical Information

### Electricity and Adapters
- **Voltage:** 230V (higher than US 110V - check device compatibility)
- **Frequency:** 50Hz
- **Plug Type:** Type C and E (two round prongs)
- **Adapters:** US/UK devices need adapters (buy at airport or electronics shops)

### Internet and WiFi
- **WiFi:** Free at many cafes, parks, libraries, McDonald's
- **Public WiFi:** "Paris Wi-Fi" network at parks and public buildings (registration required)
- **SIM Cards:** Tourist SIMs available at airports, tobacco shops (€20-40 for 7-30 days)
- **Phone Plans:** Orange, SFR, Bouygues (prepaid options)
- **Coverage:** Excellent 4G/5G throughout Paris

### Money and Currency
- **Currency:** Euro (€), coins (1c-€2), notes (€5-€500)
- **ATMs:** Widely available, accept most international cards (check fees)
- **Credit Cards:** Widely accepted (Visa/Mastercard, Amex less common)
- **Chip and PIN:** Required, some places don't accept signature cards
- **Exchange:** Avoid airport/tourist area exchanges (poor rates), use ATMs
- **Tipping:** Cash tip preferred even if paying by card

### Business Hours
- **Shops:** Generally 10:00-19:00, closed Sundays (exceptions: Marais, Champs-Élysées)
- **Restaurants:** Lunch 12:00-14:30, Dinner 19:00-22:00 (some kitchens close between)
- **Museums:** Usually 9:00-18:00, many closed Mondays or Tuesdays
- **Groceries:** Small shops daily until 21:00-23:00, supermarkets closed Sundays
- **Banks:** Monday-Friday 9:00-17:00, closed weekends

### Useful Apps
- **RATP:** Official Paris transport app (routes, schedules, tickets)
- **Citymapper:** Multi-modal transport planner (excellent for Paris)
- **Google Maps:** Navigation and walking directions
- **The Fork:** Restaurant reservations and deals (French version of OpenTable)
- **Too Good To Go:** Surplus food from restaurants at discount prices
- **Vélib':** Official bike share app

### Public Holidays
- **January 1:** New Year's Day
- **Easter Monday:** Variable (March/April)
- **May 1:** Labor Day (most things closed)
- **May 8:** Victory in Europe Day
- **July 14:** Bastille Day (fireworks at Eiffel Tower, military parade)
- **August 15:** Assumption of Mary
- **November 1:** All Saints' Day
- **November 11:** Armistice Day
- **December 25:** Christmas Day

### Museum Free Days
- **First Sunday of Month:** Many museums free (Louvre, d'Orsay, Rodin, etc.)
- **Under 18:** Free entry to most national museums
- **EU Residents Under 26:** Free entry to national museums
- **Paris Museum Pass:** Unlimited entry to 60+ museums (€62-€78 for 2-6 days)

## Day Trips from Paris

### Palace of Versailles
- **Distance:** 20 km southwest (40-60 min by train)
- **Highlights:** Opulent palace, Hall of Mirrors, extensive gardens, Marie Antoinette's Estate
- **Transport:** RER C to Versailles-Château-Rive-Gauche (€7.60 round trip)
- **Duration:** Full day recommended (palace 2-3 hours, gardens 2-4 hours)
- **Tip:** Book timed entry online, arrive at opening, avoid Tuesdays (closed Mondays)

### Giverny (Monet's Gardens)
- **Distance:** 75 km northwest (1.5 hours by train+bus)
- **Highlights:** Claude Monet's house and gardens, water lilies
- **Transport:** Train to Vernon, then shuttle bus (€30-40 round trip total)
- **Duration:** Half day (gardens 2-3 hours)
- **Season:** April-October only (gardens closed winter)

### Château de Fontainebleau
- **Distance:** 65 km southeast (1 hour by train)
- **Highlights:** Renaissance palace, Napoleon's apartments, forest
- **Transport:** Train from Gare de Lyon to Fontainebleau-Avon (€18 round trip)
- **Duration:** Full day (palace + forest)

### Champagne Region (Reims/Épernay)
- **Distance:** 150 km east (45 min-2 hours by train)
- **Highlights:** Champagne house tours (Moët, Veuve Clicquot, Taittinger), cathedral
- **Transport:** TGV train from Gare de l'Est (€40-60 round trip)
- **Duration:** Full day or overnight

## Budget Guide

### Daily Budget Estimates

**Budget Traveler (€60-100/day):**
- Accommodation: Hostel (€25-40 for dorm bed)
- Meals: Bakery breakfast, market lunch, cheap dinner (€20-30)
- Transport: Métro day pass or 10-ticket carnet (€8-10)
- Activities: Free attractions, parks, one paid museum (€10-20)

**Mid-Range Traveler (€150-250/day):**
- Accommodation: 2-3 star hotel or Airbnb (€80-120)
- Meals: Café breakfast, bistro lunch, nice restaurant dinner (€50-80)
- Transport: Métro/occasional taxi (€10-20)
- Activities: Museums, attractions, tours (€30-50)

**Luxury Traveler (€400+/day):**
- Accommodation: 4-5 star hotel (€250-500+)
- Meals: Fine dining, Michelin restaurants, wine (€150-300+)
- Transport: Taxis/private car (€30-50)
- Activities: Private tours, exclusive experiences (€100-200+)

### Money-Saving Tips
1. **Paris Museum Pass:** Pays for itself in 2-3 museums, skip ticket lines
2. **Picnic:** Buy at markets/supermarkets, eat in parks (€8-15 vs €30-50 restaurant)
3. **Free Attractions:** Many churches, parks, and neighborhoods cost nothing
4. **Happy Hour:** 17:00-20:00 drink discounts at many bars
5. **Lunch Prix Fixe:** Same restaurants cheaper at lunch than dinner
6. **Carnet of 10:** Buying 10 Métro tickets saves €4 vs individual
7. **Free Museum Days:** First Sunday of month for many major museums
8. **Drink Tap Water:** Ask for "carafe d'eau" instead of €6 bottled water

## Final Tips

1. **Learn Basic French:** Even simple phrases show respect and improve service
2. **Slow Down:** Paris is meant to be savored, not rushed
3. **Look Up:** Beautiful architecture often above eye level
4. **Get Lost:** Wander without maps, discover hidden courtyards and cafes
5. **Museum Strategy:** Buy timed tickets online, visit less popular museums too
6. **Carry Small Bills:** Many places don't accept large notes (€50+)
7. **Bread is Life:** Fresh baguette daily, eat same day (no preservatives)
8. **Afternoon Closure:** Many small shops close 12:00-14:00 for lunch
9. **Sunday Quiet:** Many shops closed, markets in morning, restaurants still open
10. **Respect the Culture:** Parisians value politeness, quietness, and effort to speak French
