# Bangkok Travel Guide

## Quick Facts

- **Country**: Thailand
- **Language**: Thai (English widely spoken in tourist areas)
- **Currency**: Thai Baht (THB, ฿)
- **Time Zone**: Indochina Time (ICT, UTC+7, no daylight saving)
- **Emergency Number**: 191 (tourist police), 1669 (ambulance)
- **Dialing Code**: +66
- **Capital**: Bangkok is the capital of Thailand
- **Population**: ~10.5 million (metropolitan area ~16 million)

## Visa Requirements

### Entry Rules by Nationality

Thailand offers visa-exempt entry for many nationalities:

- **US Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism up to **60 days** (extended from 30 days in 2024)
- **UK Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism up to **60 days**
- **Canadian Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism up to **60 days**
- **Australian Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism up to **60 days**
- **EU Citizens**: Visa-free for tourism up to **60 days**
- **Other Nationals**: Many countries eligible for visa-on-arrival (15-30 days) at airports
- **Check Latest**: Visit [Thai Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://www.mfa.go.th/) for current requirements

### Important Requirements

- **Passport Validity**: Must be valid for at least **6 months** from date of entry
- **Return Ticket**: Immigration may ask to see proof of onward travel
- **Accommodation Proof**: May request hotel booking confirmation
- **Cash**: May ask to show 20,000 THB per person (rarely enforced but carry some cash)
- **Extensions**: Can extend stay by 30 days at immigration office (1,900 THB fee)

### Visa Types for Longer Stays

- **Tourist Visa**: 60 days (extendable), apply at Thai embassy before travel
- **Special Tourist Visa (STV)**: 90 days (can extend to 270 days)
- **Education Visa**: For studying Thai language or other courses
- **Work Permit**: Required for employment (employer sponsors)

## Best Time to Visit

### Cool and Dry Season (November - February)

- **Weather**: Pleasant (20-32°C / 68-90°F), low humidity, minimal rain
- **Pros**: Best weather, festivals (Loy Krathong in November), comfortable for sightseeing
- **Cons**: Peak tourist season, higher prices, crowded attractions
- **Best for**: First-time visitors, temple sightseeing, rooftop bars

### Hot Season (March - May)

- **Weather**: Very hot (28-40°C / 82-104°F), dry, intense sun
- **Pros**: Fewer tourists, lower prices, Songkran water festival (mid-April)
- **Cons**: Oppressive heat (especially April), exhausting for outdoor activities
- **Best for**: Budget travelers, Songkran festival experience, indoor attractions

### Rainy Season / Monsoon (June - October)

- **Weather**: Hot and humid (25-35°C / 77-95°F), frequent afternoon/evening showers
- **Pros**: Lowest prices, lush greenery, short bursts of rain (not all day), manageable with planning
- **Cons**: Occasional flooding, humidity, less ideal for outdoor activities
- **Best for**: Budget travelers, shopping, food tours (covered markets)

**Peak Season**: November-February (book accommodation early)
**Shoulder Season**: March, October
**Low Season**: June-September

**Pro Tip**: Even during rainy season, mornings are often clear. Plan outdoor activities early, shopping/museums for afternoon.

### Insider Tips

- **Dress Code**: Temples require covered shoulders and knees (pants/long skirts). Bring a sarong or rent at entrance (20-50 THB).
- **Temple Hopping**: Grand Palace → Wat Pho (5-min walk) → Wat Arun (ferry across river, 5 THB) makes efficient morning route.
- **Avoid Scams**: Near Grand Palace, tuk-tuk drivers will say it's "closed today" and offer tour - ignore and enter anyway.
- **Free Ferries**: Cross river on local ferries (3-5 THB) instead of tourist boats (50+ THB).

## Neighborhoods & Districts

### Rattanakosin (Old City)

- **Character**: Historic heart, royal palaces, temples, Bangkok's original settlement
- **Must-See**: Grand Palace, Wat Pho, Khao San Road, National Museum
- **Food**: Cheap street food, backpacker cafes, traditional Thai restaurants
- **Transport**: No metro (BTS/MRT), use taxis/tuk-tuks/boats, very walkable
- **Stay Here If**: First-time visitor wanting temples and history

### Sukhumvit

- **Character**: Modern, cosmopolitan, expat hub, malls, international restaurants
- **Must-See**: Terminal 21 mall, Benjakitti Park, nightlife (Soi 11, Soi Cowboy)
- **Food**: Every cuisine imaginable, fine dining, rooftop bars, street food
- **Transport**: BTS Skytrain (Nana, Asok, Phrom Phong stations), very convenient
- **Stay Here If**: You want modern Bangkok with excellent transport

### Silom

- **Character**: Financial district by day, nightlife hub after hours, LGBTQ+ friendly
- **Must-See**: Lumpini Park, rooftop bars (Sky Bar, Octave), Patpong night market
- **Food**: Business lunch deals, rooftop dining, street food on Convent Road
- **Transport**: BTS (Sala Daeng), MRT (Silom, Lumpini)
- **Stay Here If**: You want central location with nightlife and business amenities

### Khao San Road Area

- **Character**: Legendary backpacker street, budget travel hub, 24/7 party zone
- **Must-See**: The street itself (experience!), nearby temples, Phra Athit Road (quieter)
- **Food**: Cheap Thai, Western backpacker food, street stalls, pad thai at midnight
- **Transport**: No BTS/MRT nearby (deliberate choice by area!), taxis/tuk-tuks, river boats
- **Stay Here If**: Budget traveler, want backpacker social scene, close to temples

### Thonburi (West Bank)

- **Character**: Quieter, more local, residential, fewer tourists, traditional Bangkok
- **Must-See**: Wat Arun, canal tours (khlongs), Artist's House, local markets
- **Food**: Authentic Thai food, family restaurants, less touristy
- **Transport**: Ferry across river, MRT Blue Line extension, buses
- **Stay Here If**: You want authentic local experience away from tourist crowds

### Chinatown (Yaowarat)

- **Character**: Chaotic, colorful, bustling, gold shops, incredible street food
- **Must-See**: Yaowarat Road at night, Sampeng Market, Wat Traimit (Golden Buddha)
- **Food**: Best street food in Bangkok, Chinese-Thai fusion, seafood, dim sum
- **Transport**: MRT (Wat Mangkon, Hua Lamphong), walk from Rattanakosin
- **Visit For**: Food tour (must!), market shopping, cultural immersion

## Food & Dining

### Must-Try Thai Dishes

- **Pad Thai**: Stir-fried rice noodles with tamarind sauce, egg, peanuts, lime
- **Tom Yum Goong**: Hot and sour soup with prawns, lemongrass, galangal
- **Som Tam**: Spicy green papaya salad (tell them "mai pet" for less spicy!)
- **Massaman Curry**: Rich, mild curry with peanuts, potatoes, beef or chicken
- **Khao Pad**: Thai fried rice with egg, vegetables, meat
- **Mango Sticky Rice**: Sweet coconut rice with ripe mango (dessert heaven)
- **Satay**: Grilled meat skewers with peanut sauce
- **Pad Krapow Moo Saap**: Holy basil stir-fry with pork and fried egg
- **Boat Noodles**: Small bowls of rich, flavorful noodle soup

### Where to Eat

#### Street Food (Must Experience!)

- **Price**: 40-100 THB per dish
- **Where**: Yaowarat (Chinatown), Khao San Road, Sukhumvit Soi 38, Ari neighborhood
- **Safety**: Choose stalls with high turnover (fresh ingredients), cooked-to-order
- **Best**: Don't fear the street stalls - often better and more authentic than restaurants

#### Food Courts

- **Price**: 50-150 THB per meal
- **Where**: MBK Center, Terminal 21, Siam Paragon
- **How**: Buy prepaid card, order from stalls, refund unused credit
- **Benefit**: A/C, clean, variety, cheap, tourist-friendly

#### Restaurants

- **Budget**: 100-200 THB (local restaurants, no A/C)
- **Mid-Range**: 300-600 THB (nice Thai restaurants, international food)
- **Fine Dining**: 1,500+ THB (Michelin-starred, rooftop restaurants)

#### Night Markets

- **Rod Fai Market**: Hipster vintage market with food stalls
- **Talad Rot Fai Srinakarin**: Huge night market, local crowd, great food
- **Asiatique**: Touristy but scenic riverside setting

### Dining Customs & Etiquette

- **Spice Levels**: Thai food can be extremely spicy! Say "mai pet" (not spicy) or "pet nit noi" (a little spicy)
- **Sharing**: Thai meals are meant to be shared - order variety and share with table
- **Fork & Spoon**: Use spoon as main utensil (right hand), fork to push food onto spoon (chopsticks for noodles)
- **Tipping**: Not traditionally expected, but appreciated. Round up or leave 20-40 THB, high-end restaurants 10%
- **Water**: Don't drink tap water. Bottled water everywhere (7-10 THB at 7-Eleven)
- **Ice**: Generally safe in restaurants/stalls with high turnover
- **Halal/Vegetarian**: Say "gin jeh" for vegetarian, "halal" widely available especially in Muslim areas

### Street Food Safety Tips

- **Choose Wisely**: High customer turnover = fresh ingredients
- **Cooked to Order**: Safer than pre-cooked sitting out
- **Hot Food**: Thoroughly cooked and served hot is safest
- **Avoid**: Raw vegetables if you have a sensitive stomach (first few days)
- **Stick to**: Grilled, fried, boiled dishes

## Transportation

### From Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK)

- **Airport Rail Link (ARL)**: To Phaya Thai BTS station (30 min, 45 THB) or Makkasan (City Air Terminal)
- **Taxi**: Metered from official stand on level 1 (250-400 THB + 50 THB airport surcharge + tolls ~75 THB), 45-90 min depending on traffic
- **Grab**: Similar to taxi, order from arrivals level
- **Limousine Services**: More expensive but convenient (1,000+ THB)
- **Bus**: Cheap but slow (35-60 THB, 1-2 hours)

### From Don Mueang Airport (DMK)

- **Bus**: A1, A2 to BTS/MRT stations (30 THB)
- **Taxi**: Metered (200-350 THB + airport surcharge + tolls)
- **Train**: Free train to Don Mueang station, then train to Hua Lamphong (slow, cheap)

### BTS Skytrain

- **Coverage**: Sukhumvit Line (light green) and Silom Line (dark green)
- **Hours**: 6:00-24:00
- **Tickets**: 17-65 THB depending on distance, day pass 140 THB, Rabbit Card (stored value)
- **Best For**: Sukhumvit, Siam, Silom areas - fast, efficient, A/C

### MRT (Metro)

- **Coverage**: Blue Line (loop), Purple Line (north), Yellow Line (east)
- **Hours**: 6:00-24:00
- **Tickets**: 16-42 THB, day pass 120-140 THB, stored value card
- **Best For**: Connecting BTS gaps, Chinatown, Chatuchak Market

### Taxis & Ride-Sharing

- **Metered Taxis**: Abundant, starting fare 35 THB, ~7 THB per km
- **Always Use Meter**: Say "meter, please" or "by meter" - refuse fixed price offers
- **Grab**: Ride-hailing app, shows price upfront, convenient, widely used
- **Bolt**: Similar to Grab, competitive pricing
- **Traffic**: Avoid rush hours (7-9am, 5-8pm) - can double journey time

### Tuk-Tuks

- **Iconic**: Three-wheeled motorized rickshaw, quintessential Bangkok experience
- **Always Negotiate**: No meter, agree price before getting in
- **Price Guide**: Short trip 50-100 THB, longer 100-200 THB (usually more expensive than taxi!)
- **When**: Fun for short trips, tourist experience, not practical for daily transport
- **Scams**: Drivers offer "20 THB tours" then take you to gem shops for commission - politely decline

### Boats & Ferries

- **Chao Phraya Express Boat**: River ferry (10-40 THB), tourist boat (60 THB all-day pass)
- **Khlong Boats**: Canal boats (9-19 THB), cheap and scenic but chaotic for first-timers
- **Cross-River Ferries**: 3-5 THB, connect both banks
- **Best For**: Avoiding traffic, scenic travel, reaching riverside attractions

### Motorcycle Taxis

- **Orange Vests**: Drivers wear numbered orange vests at designated stands
- **Short Trips**: Navigate through traffic, soi (alley) shortcuts
- **Price**: 10-40 THB for very short distances
- **Safety**: No helmets often, use at your own risk, not for tourists with large bags

### Traffic Warning

- **Rush Hour**: 7-9am, 5-8pm - absolutely avoid taxis during these times
- **Alternative**: Use BTS/MRT during rush hour, much faster
- **Friday Evening**: Worst traffic of the week

## Cultural Tips & Customs

### Respect for the Monarchy

- **Lèse-majesté Laws**: Insulting the monarchy is illegal (3-15 years imprisonment)
- **Show Respect**: Stand for royal anthem in cinemas/theaters, don't criticize monarchy
- **Currency**: Never step on coins/bills (they have King's image)
- **Serious**: This is not taken lightly - tourists have been arrested

### Buddhist Customs

- **Temples**: Remove shoes before entering, dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered)
- **Buddha Images**: Never climb on, point feet at, or touch disrespectfully
- **Monks**: Women cannot touch monks or hand things directly (place on table)
- **Photos**: Ask before photographing monks or during ceremonies

### The Wai Greeting

- **What**: Press palms together at chest, slight bow
- **When**: Greet elders, show respect, thank someone
- **Don't Overdo**: Not expected with service staff receiving payment, vendors, or equals
- **Return It**: If someone wais you, wai back (except children)

### Head and Feet

- **Head is Sacred**: Don't touch anyone's head (including children)
- **Feet are Lowest**: Don't point feet at people, Buddha images, or monks
- **Sitting**: Tuck feet under when sitting on floor in temples
- **Stepping Over**: Walk around people, not over them

### Dress & Appearance

- **Temples**: Shoulders and knees covered mandatory (sarongs available at entrance)
- **Grand Palace**: Strictly enforced - no shorts, sleeveless tops, torn jeans
- **General**: Dress neatly; Thais judge by appearance (shabby dress = less respect)
- **Beach Wear**: For beaches only, not for walking around city
- **Shoes**: Remove before entering homes, some shops, temples

### Social Etiquette

- **Mai Pen Rai**: "Never mind" / "no worries" - Thai attitude of letting go
- **Sanuk**: Fun is important - Thais prioritize enjoyment and smile
- **Sabai Sabai**: Take it easy, don't stress
- **Kreng Jai**: Consideration for others' feelings, avoid confrontation
- **Losing Face**: Raising voice or showing anger = losing face (highly negative)
- **Smile**: "Land of Smiles" for a reason - smile often, especially when asking for help

### Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Remove shoes before entering temples and homes
- Dress modestly at temples
- Show respect for Buddha images and monks
- Smile and stay calm in all situations
- Learn basic Thai phrases ("sawatdee krap/ka", "kop khun krap/ka")

**DON'T:**
- Touch anyone's head
- Point feet at people or Buddha images
- Step over people or food
- Raise your voice or show anger
- Wear shoes inside temples/homes
- Criticize the monarchy (seriously!)

### Language Tips

- **Sawatdee krap/ka**: Hello (men say "krap", women say "ka")
- **Kop khun krap/ka**: Thank you
- **Mai pen rai**: No problem / You're welcome
- **Chai**: Yes
- **Mai**: No
- **Aroi**: Delicious
- **Tao rai?**: How much?
- **Bin, please**: Bill, please (in restaurants)
- **Mai pet**: Not spicy
- **Phet nit noi**: A little spicy

## Safety & Health

### Safety Overview

Bangkok is generally safe for tourists, but petty crime (pickpocketing, scams) exists in tourist areas.

### Safe City But Stay Alert

- **Violent Crime**: Rare against tourists
- **Petty Crime**: Pickpocketing in crowded areas (markets, BTS during rush hour)
- **Bag Snatching**: Occasional, especially on motorcycles - wear crossbody bags
- **Late Night**: Generally safe, but use common sense (avoid dark alleys alone)

### Common Scams

- **Gem Scam**: Friendly stranger suggests "duty-free gem store" or "special deal today" - massive scam, avoid
- **Tuk-Tuk Tours**: "20 THB tour" ends at tailors/gem shops where driver gets commission
- **Grand Palace Closed**: Scammers say palace is closed, offer alternative tour - ignore and enter
- **Jet Ski Scam**: At Pattaya beaches, operators claim damage and demand huge payment
- **Taxi Not Using Meter**: Always insist on meter, walk away if they refuse
- **Overcharging**: Count change, verify prices (especially in touristy areas)
- **Friendly Local**: Overly helpful strangers may lead to scam - polite but cautious

### Transportation Safety

- **Official Taxis**: Use metered taxis from stands or Grab app
- **Motorcycle Taxis**: Risky (no helmets often), avoid if risk-averse
- **Crossing Streets**: Traffic doesn't always stop - cross at lights, use pedestrian bridges
- **Tuk-Tuks**: Hang on tight, no seatbelts, thrilling but not safest

### Health & Medical

- **Hospitals**: Excellent private hospitals (Bumrungrad, Bangkok Hospital) - expensive but high quality
- **Insurance**: Essential - medical tourism common, costs can be high
- **Pharmacies**: 7-Eleven, Boots, Watson's everywhere - many medications available over-counter
- **Travel Vaccines**: Consult doctor - Hepatitis A, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended
- **Dengue**: Mosquito-borne disease, use repellent (especially rainy season)
- **Air Quality**: Can be poor, check AQI if sensitive

### Food & Water

- **Tap Water**: Not drinkable - bottled water everywhere (cheap)
- **Ice**: Generally safe in restaurants/stalls with high turnover
- **Street Food**: Safe if cooked fresh with high turnover
- **Traveler's Tummy**: Common first few days - bring Imodium, stay hydrated
- **Spicy Food**: Can upset stomach - start mild, increase gradually

### Heat & Sun

- **Hot Year-Round**: Stay hydrated, drink 3+ liters water daily
- **Heat Exhaustion**: Real risk - seek A/C, drink electrolytes, rest in heat of day
- **Sunscreen**: Essential, high SPF, reapply often
- **A/C Escape**: Malls, BTS/MRT are A/C sanctuaries

### Emergency Numbers

- **Tourist Police**: 1155 (English spoken)
- **General Police**: 191
- **Ambulance**: 1669
- **Fire**: 199
- **Embassy**: Keep your country's contact info

## Practical Information

### Electricity

- **Voltage**: 220V, 50Hz
- **Plug Types**: Type A (2-pin flat), Type B (2-pin flat + ground), Type C (2-pin round)
- **Adapters**: Often not needed for US/EU plugs (most sockets accept both)
- **Where to Buy**: 7-Eleven, airports, electronics malls (Pantip Plaza)

### Money & Banking

- **Currency**: Thai Baht (THB, ฿)
- **ATMs**: Everywhere, withdraw 20,000-30,000 THB max per transaction
- **ATM Fees**: 220 THB per withdrawal + your bank's fee (expensive!)
- **Exchange**: Super Rich (orange or green branches), better rates than banks
- **Credit Cards**: Accepted at malls, hotels, upscale restaurants; not at street food
- **Cash King**: Most places prefer cash, carry 500-1,000 THB daily
- **Daily Budget**: Budget 1,000-1,500 THB, mid-range 2,500-4,000 THB, luxury 6,000+ THB

### 7-Eleven Ubiquity

- **Everywhere**: Literally every corner, open 24/7
- **Services**: ATM, snacks, drinks, toiletries, SIM cards, bill payment
- **Reliable**: Clean bathrooms (some locations), A/C, consistent prices
- **Pro Tip**: Your go-to for water, snacks, and emergency supplies

### Pharmacies

- **Chains**: Boots, Watson's, Fascino in malls
- **7-Eleven**: Basic medications
- **Over-Counter**: Many prescription drugs available without prescription
- **Cost**: Cheap compared to Western countries

### SIM Cards & Internet

- **Providers**: AIS, TrueMove, DTAC
- **Tourist SIM**: 200-500 THB for 7-30 days with data
- **Where to Buy**: Airport (convenient but pricier), 7-Eleven, provider shops
- **Data**: 10-20 GB typical for tourist SIM, unlimited social media
- **WiFi**: Free in malls, cafes, hotels (ask for password)

### Tap Water

- **Don't Drink**: Not safe for foreigners
- **Brushing Teeth**: Generally okay, some use bottled to be safe
- **Showering**: Completely safe
- **Bottles**: 7-10 THB at 7-Eleven for 600ml

### Business Hours

- **Shops**: 10:00-20:00 or 21:00 (malls open until 22:00)
- **Street Food**: Early morning to late night (varies by location)
- **Temples**: Usually 8:00-17:00 or 18:00
- **7-Eleven**: 24/7 (some smaller ones close midnight)

### Useful Apps

- **Grab/Bolt**: Ride-hailing (essential)
- **Google Maps**: Navigation, works well in Bangkok
- **Google Translate**: Camera translate for Thai script
- **XE Currency**: Exchange rates
- **Hungry Hub**: Restaurant reservations with discounts
- **BKK Delivery Apps**: GrabFood, FoodPanda, LINE MAN

### Public Toilets

- **Malls**: Free, clean, Western-style
- **BTS/MRT**: Some stations have toilets (5 THB)
- **7-Eleven**: Some have toilets (hit or miss)
- **Temples**: Often available (donation appreciated)
- **Toilet Paper**: Carry tissues (not always provided, bin not flush)
- **Squat Toilets**: Common outside tourist areas

### Shopping

- **Malls**: Siam Paragon, CentralWorld, MBK, Terminal 21, Emporium
- **Markets**: Chatuchak (weekend), Rod Fai (night), Sampeng (Chinatown wholesale)
- **Bargaining**: Expected at markets (not malls), start at 50% of asking price
- **VAT Refund**: Spend 2,000+ THB at VAT Refund shops, claim at airport (7% refund)

### Festivals

- **Songkran (mid-April)**: Water festival / Thai New Year - massive water fight, banks/offices closed 3-5 days
- **Loy Krathong (November)**: Floating lanterns and baskets on water, beautiful
- **Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb)**: Chinatown celebrations

---

**Travel Responsibly**: Be respectful of Thai culture, especially regarding monarchy and Buddhism. Support local businesses, avoid elephant riding (unethical tourism), and dress modestly at temples.

**Pro Tip**: Bangkok rewards those who explore beyond the main sights. Wander the sois (alleys) in Sukhumvit, take a khlong boat tour, eat where there's no English menu, and chat with locals. The real Bangkok reveals itself when you venture off the beaten path.
